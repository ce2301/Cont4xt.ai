The AI Lifecycle Gap

Most AI adoption fails for the same three reasons.

No lifecycle before deployment

Teams ship AI pilots with no strategy for what comes before — the product lifecycle decisions that determine whether an AI system creates durable value or becomes a liability to manage.

No framework inside development

Engineering teams build AI capabilities without a structured development methodology — creating compounding risk, technical debt, and brittle systems that work in demos but fail in production.

No discipline after launch

Post-deployment AI — model drift, incident response, production monitoring, accountability — is treated as an afterthought. Most organizations have no framework for what happens after go-live.

These aren't technology problems. They're lifecycle problems. And there are frameworks built specifically to solve them.

The engineering methodology your development teams need to move from prototype to production — reliably, repeatably, and with the rigor enterprise AI demands. No more brittle handoffs.

The post-deployment discipline most organizations skip entirely. How to operate, monitor, and maintain AI systems in production — and what to do when they fail. You've shipped AI. Now you need to run it.

  • Continuous improvement loops
